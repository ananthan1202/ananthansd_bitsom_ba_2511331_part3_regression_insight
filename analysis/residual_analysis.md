# Residual Analysis

## Final Model Used
Multiple Linear Regression

## Predicted Sales

Predicted sales were calculated using the multiple regression equation generated in Excel.

## Residual Calculation

Residual = Actual Monthly Sales − Predicted Monthly Sales

Positive residuals indicate that actual sales were higher than predicted.

Negative residuals indicate that actual sales were lower than predicted.

## Largest Positive Residuals

The five records with the largest positive residuals represent stores that performed significantly better than the model predicted. Possible reasons include:

- Successful local promotions
- Seasonal demand
- Strong store management
- Local events increasing customer visits
- Customer loyalty

## Largest Negative Residuals

The five records with the largest negative residuals represent stores that performed worse than predicted. Possible reasons include:

- Stock shortages
- Local competition
- Operational issues
- Reduced customer demand
- External economic factors

## Business Interpretation

The residuals indicate that while the regression model explains most of the variation in monthly sales, some stores are influenced by additional factors that were not included in the model.

## Under- or Over-Prediction

- Positive residuals suggest the model under-predicted sales.
- Negative residuals suggest the model over-predicted sales.

Overall, the multiple regression model performs well (R² ≈ 0.81), but there are still unexplained differences due to factors outside the available dataset.
